Frank Sinatra

Frank Sinatra, also known as "Ol' Blue Eyes" and "The Chairman of the Board," was one of the most influential and popular musical artists of the 20th century. His notable songs include "My Way," "Fly Me to the Moon," and "New York, New York." He won nine Grammy Awards and sold over 150 million records worldwide.
1915-1998

Elvis Presley

Known as the "King of Rock and Roll," Elvis Presley is regarded as one of the most significant cultural icons of the 20th century. His notable songs include "Heartbreak Hotel," "Hound Dog," and "Jailhouse Rock." He won three Grammy Awards and starred in numerous films.
1935-1977
